#### 作文 1：Smart Campus 倡议建议信（13/20）

- **日期**：2026年7月14日
- **来源**：53英语练习
- **题目**：2025北京高考英语作文（满分20分）

> 假设你是红星中学高三学生李华。联合国正面向全球青少年开展倡议征集活动。你的外国好友Jim打算参加，为此发来邮件，就倡议内容询问你的建议。请你用英文给他回复，内容包括：
> 1. 提出的建议；
> 2. 建议的理由。
> 提示词：倡议 proposal

**你的原文**：

> Dear Jim,
> I'm glad to know that your school is recruiting the proposal of the Smart Campus. As a student who is enjoying the benefit from the technology, it's my plesure to let you know how to write your proposal.
> The most important thing of the Smart Campus is smart dining house. It clever to use applications to book the meal before the eating, it can help the organizer easily to know what amount and which kind of meal should they prepare, nicely to reduce the food waste.
> Hope this can help you to finish your proposal, if you have the time, fell free to our school to see how the Smart Campus running.
> Yours,
> Li Hua

**评分明细**：

| 维度 | 得分 | 满分 | 说明 |
|------|:---:|:---:|------|
| 内容覆盖 | 6 | 8 | 倡议方向正确，但仅覆盖智能食堂一个点，缺少展开和广度 |
| 语言质量 | 4 | 7 | 8处语法/拼写错误，词汇有限，句式单一 |
| 结构连贯 | 3 | 5 | 基本框架有，但段间衔接和逻辑连贯性不足 |
| **总分** | **13** | **20** | 三档偏中 |

**错因逐条分析**：

**一、内容层面**：

1. **内容单一**：仅提了"智能食堂"一个点。倡议书面向联合国全球青少年，应涵盖更全面的Smart Campus愿景，如智能教学、绿色校园、数字管理、无纸化学习等。

2. **理由展开不足**："reduce food waste"是一个好的理由，但一句话带过，没有展开说明如何减少、减少到什么程度、为什么重要。

**二、语法层面**：

| 原文错误 | 错误类型 | 修正 |
|------|:---:|------|
| It clever to use | 缺 be 动词 | It **is** clever to use |
| what amount and which kind of meal should they prepare | 间接疑问句不倒装 | ...what amount and which kind of meal **they should** prepare |
| how the Smart Campus running | 缺 be 动词 | how the Smart Campus **is** running |
| nicely to reduce | 副词使用不当 | **effectively reducing** / **thus reducing** |
| the benefit from the technology | 介词不当 | the benefit**s of** technology |

**三、拼写层面**：

| 错误 | 正确 |
|------|------|
| plesure | plea**s**ure |
| fell free | fe**e**l free |
| dining house | dining **hall** / **canteen** |

**参考范文（三档→四档跨越的关键对比）**：

> Dear Jim,
> I'm glad to know that you're preparing a proposal for the UN's youth initiative. As a student benefiting from smart campus technology, I'd like to share some ideas with you.
>
> I suggest you focus on the "Smart Canteen System". First, by using an app to pre-order meals, students can save time queuing and the canteen can prepare the right amount of food accordingly, effectively reducing food waste. Second, the system can analyze students' dietary habits and offer personalized nutrition advice, promoting healthier eating.
>
> Besides the canteen, you could also mention smart classrooms and digital campus management in your proposal to make it more comprehensive. A smart campus not only improves efficiency but also contributes to sustainable development.
>
> Hope these suggestions help. Feel free to visit our school and see how it works in practice.
> Yours,
> Li Hua

**提升要点**：

1. 内容至少覆盖2-3个点，每个点配1-2句理由展开
2. 检查清单：be动词是否遗漏？间接疑问句是否倒装？拼写（pleasure/feel）？介词搭配（benefits of）？

### 错题 1：完形填空 第3题

- **录入时间**：2026年7月14日
- **来源**：53英语，《三年模拟》2025届北京通州期中，完形填空
- **文章主题**：个人成长 — "Failing Successfully"

**完整题干（文章全文）**：

> Failing Successfully
>
> Two years ago I took part in a speech competition and it was a hard time for me when the judge announced the winners. At last, the moment of truth came. I got the worst.
>
> However, I really wanted to try again. I realized that finding the right topic was the most important part of the whole process. It needs to be motivational and inspirational to the audience. So I reworked my speech for the following year, **picking** a different topic and spending many hours making it flow better.
>
> The next year I participated in the competition again. I gave my speech in two parts, one was about my own experience and the other addressed the common feelings people have when giving a speech—how frightening it is, standing on a stage all alone, with everyone sitting and watching them. My speech went off well and I was hoping to do that a little bit better. Sadly, my wish didn't come true. The competition was so fierce and again I wasn't placed.
>
> I was deeply disappointed, since I couldn't accept the fact that I had failed twice in something that I had worked so hard on. However, I knew that losing is usual and part of life. One of the judges advised me to perfect my gestures and delivery. He said that I would surely succeed someday.
>
> While I didn't win awards in the competition, I did gain a new viewpoint. I discovered that I could fail successfully. Now, whenever I'm faced with a defeat, I remind myself of what a famous person said, "The path was worn and slippery. My foot slipped from under me, knocking the other out of the way, but I recovered and said to myself that it's a slip and not a fall."

**错误题目**：

> 3. So I reworked my speech for the following year, ___ a different topic and spending many hours making it flow better.
>
> A. picking　　B. editing　　C. reviewing　　D. comparing

**正确答案**：A (picking)

**错误原因**：对 pick 表"挑选"在上下文中的用法不够敏感。此处语境为"重新准备演讲稿，___一个不同的主题"，pick a different topic = 挑选一个不同的主题，是最自然的搭配。editing（编辑/修改）虽然也有"改动"之意，但通常搭配 editing an article/speech/video，侧重对已有内容的修改，而非重新选择主题。reviewing（复习/回顾）和 comparing（比较）与上下文不符。

**词汇积累**：
- `pick a topic` — 挑选主题（pick 在此为日常高频用法的延伸）
- `rework` — 重新加工、修改
- `flow` —（文章/演讲）流畅

**同类题警示**：完形中动词的辨析不能只看中文释义，要关注英文搭配习惯（collocation）。

- **录入时间**：2026年7月14日

**错误题目**：

> 8. One of the judges advised me to ___ my gestures and delivery.
>
> A. assume　　B. reflect　　C. perfect　　D. acquire

**正确答案**：C (perfect)

**错误原因**：**熟词生义**。perfect 常见义为形容词"完美的"，此处用作动词，意为"使完美、完善"。评委建议作者"完善手势和表达方式" —— perfect my gestures and delivery。这是高考高频考点：常见形容词转用作动词。

**词汇积累**：
- `perfect` v. 使完美、完善 → *perfect one's skills/technique/gestures*
- `delivery` n. 此处指"演讲的表达方式、呈现"，非"快递"
- `gesture` n. 手势、姿态

### 错题 3：阅读理解 第2题

- **录入时间**：2026年7月14日
- **来源**：53英语，《三年模拟》2025届北京海淀期中，阅读理解C篇
- **文章主题**：认识自我 — 认知任务的难度感知

**完整题干（文章全文）**：

> For decades, scientists hypothesized that cognitively demanding tasks, such as writing or solving math problems, require more energy than easier tasks like scrolling short videos on the phone or looking out of the window. However, recent research proves the view wrong. Our brains perform massively complex and metabolically costly computations all day long. "Whether you're engaged in a cognitively demanding task at work, or you're staring off into space, your brain is using roughly the same amount of energy," says neuroscientist Todd Braver of Washington University in St. Louis.
>
> In fact, what feels more challenging is when a task requires the brain to go against its usual habits. Cognitively demanding work often requires the coordination of many different brain regions in novel patterns, while silencing circuits that frequently fire together.
>
> Studies have found that pushing against our brain's habits triggers an emotional response, making us feel uncomfortable. "It's aversive, and it just feels bad," says Michael Inzlicht, a psychologist from the University of Toronto. But he points out that emotional responses are subjective: They are interpretations of sensations in our bodies and brains. And we can influence how much we pay attention to these sensations and even how we interpret them. We often overestimate the struggle and pain required to complete cognitive tasks. This initial overestimation can lead us to procrastinate, or even avoid the task altogether.
>
> Studies suggest that if we can get past that initial hurdle of starting a task, then thinking becomes easier. One way to do that is to pay attention to the benefits instead of the efforts required. In a study published in the journal Science in 2020, Andrew Westbrook, a neuroscientist at Rutgers University, and his colleagues gave participants the choice of solving an easy memory puzzle for a small amount of money or a much harder puzzle for more money. The options were displayed on a screen, and participants' eye movements were tracked as they decided which puzzle to attempt. When people spent more time looking at the reward for the challenging puzzle, they were more likely to choose it. If our mind's eye, or our attentional focus, is on the benefits of an option, over time we're more likely to choose to do hard things.
>
> Performing complex mental tasks will also be less painful if the exertion of mental effort is habituated. When we don't have to make a decision to focus every day, we will save ourselves considering the costs or consequences. To make cognitively demanding tasks a habit, try to do them every day at the same time and in the same place. Add a ritual before the work starts, such as turning off the phone and launching an app to block distracting websites. After a few weeks, concentrating deeply won't just feel easier; it will actually be easier.

**错误题目**：

> 2. The author quotes the 2020 study mainly to ______.
>
> A. support a solution　　B. present a case　　C. analyze the causes　　D. challenge an assumption

**正确答案**：A (support a solution)

**错误原因**：**阅读理解功能题 —— 未准确识别引用论据的论证功能**。2020年研究所在段落的核心句是 "One way to do that is to pay attention to the benefits instead of the efforts required"（一个方法是关注收益而非付出）。紧接着引用研究，证明"多看奖励 → 更愿选难题"。该研究服务于前面提出的 solution，起到**支持论证**的作用。

易混淆项分析：
- B. present a case（呈现案例）：研究确实是一个案例，但作者引用的目的不只是"展示"，而是用它来**证明**前面提出的方法有效。功能题的落脚点在"为什么引用"，不在"引用的是什么"
- C. analyze the causes（分析原因）：该段讨论的是解决方法，不是原因分析
- D. challenge an assumption（挑战假设）：研究支持观点，不是挑战

**解题技巧 ——"引用意图题"三步法**：
1. 定位引用内容所在的段落
2. 找到引用**之前**的段落主题句（topic sentence），通常就是作者要论证的观点
3. 判断引用与主题句的关系：支持/反驳/举例/对比

本题中，引用之前的主题句是 "One way to do that is to pay attention to the benefits..."，引用在此正是为了支持这个 solution。

**长难句拆解**：
> When people spent more time looking at the reward for the challenging puzzle, they were more likely to choose it.
- 主句：they were more likely to choose it
- 时间状语从句：When people spent more time looking at the reward for the challenging puzzle
- `spend time (in) doing sth.` 结构，looking at... 是现在分词做伴随

**词汇积累**：
- `cognitively demanding` — 认知上要求高的
- `metabolically costly` — 代谢成本高的
- `aversive` — 令人厌恶的
- `procrastinate` — 拖延
- `exertion` — 努力、费力
- `habituated` — 习惯化的
- `ritual` — 仪式、习惯性步骤

#### 06 | 53英语 P249 第4题 | 2026-07-15

**来源**：53英语 P249 [14] 三年模拟（2025届北京朝阳期中，B篇）

**题型**：阅读理解 — 情感态度判断

**文章原文**：

I'm walking around the house with a severe limp(跛行) and actively second-guessing every decision I've ever made. That can only mean one thing: I just ran my one race for the year.

The 7-mile race is in my hometown in Illinois, and I've been doing it almost every year since I was a 12-year-old boy. I reached my peak about 15 years ago and have been performing poorly ever since.

Why do I keep going if I no longer enjoy it and am getting worse every year? Tradition, mostly. Besides, I don't dislike the whole race, just the running part. I like seeing all the running club members I've known since childhood, and I love the free beer afterward. Running is just a minor block on the way to the good stuff.

That day, I was accompanied to the race by a group of three extremely in-shape people. We all live in the same Indianapolis suburb. These guys bike and run countless miles, often in the same day. One has done dozens of Spartan Races, which require the player to crawl through mud, flip tires and throw spears. Another is training for a triathlon(三项全能运动). In addition to being faster than me on foot, in the water and on wheels, he can also outlift me.

I didn't hold much hope for the race—no one puts less pressure on me than me. Between miles 4 and 5, I felt so good that I wondered why I only do this once a year. Then I hit mile 6, and my body warned me. I pushed myself until I had a mile left, at which point my legs decided they were done. Determination and a can-do attitude are no match for gravity and distance. Exhausted, I slowly made my way to the finishing line.

Still, I can honestly say I gave everything I had. My slow, plodding(步履艰难的) finish was actually a victory that proves I gave it my all. I don't need a huge cup to honor my heroic performance. My aching knee is enough of a souvenir.

A day later, even as my entire body tells me I'm an idiot, I can't help but feel that the race was a success. I've decided to keep doing this race as long as my legs can support me.

**错误题目**：

4. How did the author feel after the race?

A. Surprised.  B. **Satisfied.** ✅  C. Regretful.  **D. Grateful.** ❌（你的选择）

**错误原因**：

仅凭文章末句"keep doing this race as long as my legs can support me"感觉作者对比赛"心怀感激"，选了 grateful。但通读全文，作者的情感基调是"虽然身体痛苦但心理上感到满足/不后悔"——最后一段明确说"the race was a success"，这是典型的 satisfaction（满足感），而非 gratitude（感激他人/外界）。grateful 需要明确感恩对象，文中无此依据。

**解题技巧 — 情感态度题"三步法"**：

1. **找情感锚点句**：定位文章最后一段/最后几句，这里是"the race was a success"。
2. **排除无依据选项**：grateful → 文中无感恩对象；surprised → 作者对结果并不意外；regretful → "keep doing this race"表明不后悔。
3. **用全文基调验证**：全文幽默自嘲但积极乐观（"self-deprecating yet positive"），satisfied 最贴合。

**生词积累**：

- `limp` — 跛行
- `in-shape` — 体型好的、健康的
- `outlift` — 举重超过（某人）
- `plodding` — 步履艰难的、缓慢沉重的
- `souvenir` — 纪念品（此处指膝盖酸痛成了比赛的"纪念"）
